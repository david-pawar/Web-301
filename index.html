Banff National Park

Facts: Established in 1885

Facts: 6641 sq. km. area

Specialities

Banff National Park is part of the Canadian Rocky Mountain Parks, a UNESCO World Heritage Site recognized for its outstanding natural beauty.


The park is home to a diverse range of wildlife, including grizzly bears, black bears, elk, moose, bighorn sheep, and various bird species.


Places to Visit

The town of Banff, located within the national park, is a popular tourist destination and serves as a gateway to the park. It offers amenities, accommodations, and access to outdoor activities.


Banff Upper Hot Springs, located within the park, is a popular attraction where visitors can enjoy relaxing in natural hot springs while surrounded by mountain views.

Precautions

Keep a safe distance from wildlife: Observe animals from a distance and do not approach them. This includes both predators like bears and larger herbivores like elk.

Carry bear spray: If you plan on hiking or exploring the backcountry, carry bear spray and know how to use it. Make noise to alert bears to your presence and avoid surprising them.
